Digital Divide: Digital Skills Gap

Bridging gaps through digital literacy and inclusion

Introduction

Technology is essential in modern life, but not everyone has the skills to use it effectively. This creates a digital skills gap, where individuals lack the knowledge needed to fully participate in education, work, and online activities.

As society becomes more dependent on digital tools, this gap limits opportunities and creates challenges for those with insufficient training or access to learning resources.

Vision

To create an inclusive society where everyone has equal access to digital technology and the skills needed to succeed, ensuring that digital literacy becomes a basic right for all.

Mission

To reduce the digital skills gap by providing access to digital tools, training programs, and educational resources. The project promotes collaboration with schools and communities to empower individuals with essential digital skills.

Purpose

This project aims to understand and address the lack of digital skills among students. It focuses on identifying challenges and raising awareness about digital literacy in education.

The goal is to improve students' ability to use technology effectively for academic success.

Description

The digital skills gap refers to the difference between individuals who can effectively use digital tools and those who cannot. Even with access to devices and internet, many students struggle with online platforms and digital tasks.

This issue also affects elderly and low-income communities.
